# Content Ethics

The project may be direct, hard, and serious. It must never be hateful, manipulative, or exploitative. These are the explicit boundaries.

## The principle

**Hard truth. Clean heart.**

The output should sharpen the viewer, not flatter them — and never punch down.

## What this project will not produce

- Hatred toward women, men, or any group
- Humiliation-based motivation ("you're a loser unless...")
- Alpha/beta language or hierarchy of male worth
- Racial, religious, or political scapegoating
- Glorification of violence or revenge
- Sexual coercion or exploitation framed as desire
- Manipulative fear content or fake urgency
- Conspiracy framing
- Clinical mental-health claims presented as certainty ("anxiety means X")
- Guru worship or personality-cult content
- Claims that money, conquest, or status define a person's worth

## What this project will produce

- Responsibility
- Duty
- Courage
- Restraint
- Self-command
- Mortality faced directly
- War and grief honestly portrayed
- Women's suffering in war taken seriously
- Sexuality and autonomy held as adult subjects
- Masculine discipline
- Feminine dignity
- Tragedy and consequence

## How to hold the line

The difference between hard and cruel is who pays the cost. Hard content asks the viewer to bear the cost themselves. Cruel content shifts the cost onto an out-group.

The frame to maintain:

- Hard truth **without contempt**
- Beauty **without exploitation**
- War **without glorification**
- Sexuality **without coercion**
- Masculinity **without resentment**
- Discipline **without self-hatred**

## Tests to apply before publishing

Ask of every draft:

1. **Cost test** — does this ask the viewer to do something hard themselves, or does it license them to look down on someone else?
2. **Punch-down test** — is any group being made into the cause of the viewer's problems?
3. **Manipulation test** — am I using fear, shame, or urgency to bypass the viewer's judgment?
4. **Specificity test** — am I making a clinical claim ("this is what depression is") I cannot back up?
5. **Quotability test** — would the author of the source text recognize this framing as fair to their work, even if sharpened?

If any test fails, rewrite.

## Edge cases

- **Quoting authors whose other views are objectionable.** Acceptable when the specific quote being used stands on its own and the project does not endorse the wider corpus. Add a `notes` field on the source entry flagging the issue.
- **Material about women, by men.** Treat with extra care. The frame `women_and_war`, `feminine dignity`, and `homefront_rebuilding` exists to take women seriously as historical subjects, not as backdrops for men's stories.
- **Material on sexuality.** Adult and honest is allowed; transactional, manipulative, or pickup-artist framing is not. The `sensual_autonomy` script angle is meant to belong to the subject, not the viewer.
- **War material.** Always include the cost — to the dead, to the survivors, to the homefront. Battle as spectacle without consequence is the failure state.

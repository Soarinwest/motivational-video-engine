# 01 — Content Philosophy

> **Hard truth. Clean heart.**

This document is the editorial spine of the project. Any script the engine produces — and any prompt that produces a script — should be measurable against what's written here. The [ethics check stage](04_PIPELINE_ARCHITECTURE.md) enforces it in code.

## The two-line creed

- **Hard truth.** Don't soften reality to make the viewer feel better. The work, the cost, the time, the discipline — name them honestly.
- **Clean heart.** Don't weaponize that truth. No contempt. No mockery of the weak, the late-starters, or the struggling. The viewer is a peer being addressed seriously, not a target being dunked on.

If a line passes the first test but fails the second, cut it. If it passes the second but not the first, it's filler.

## Audience

Men who respond to direct language, responsibility, discipline, restraint, competence, endurance, honor, and service. A good video should feel like a serious thought spoken by someone who respects the listener enough not to flatter him, and respects him enough not to degrade him.

## Masculine discipline without resentment

The voice is direct, calm, and load-bearing. It carries weight without shouting.

- **Direct, not abrasive.** Short sentences. Concrete nouns. No throat-clearing.
- **Earned, not performed.** Discipline is a practice, not a personality. No posturing.
- **Without enemies.** Don't build the viewer up by tearing a group down — not women, not other men, not "the modern world," not any tribe. The opponent is always the viewer's own inertia.
- **Without grievance.** Resentment is the failure mode this project most actively avoids. If a draft reads like a complaint about how unfair things are, it's wrong — regardless of how true the complaint is.

## The message is NOT

- become an alpha
- dominate everyone
- trust no one
- women are the problem
- emotion is weakness
- weakness deserves contempt
- your worth depends on money, status, or conquest

## The message IS

- govern yourself
- become useful
- keep your word
- carry your weight
- build real competence
- practice restraint
- tell the truth
- stop outsourcing blame
- master your appetites
- build a life that can carry responsibility

## Source material, not product

Draw from Stoicism, classical literature, myth, nature writing, speeches, military and exploration history, existential literature, and practical modern wisdom. **Quotes and authors are source material, not the product.** The product is interpretation, application, and clear speech. A script that is mostly someone else's words has not done its job.

## Editorial gate

Before a script advances to the voice stage, it must pass three questions:

1. **Is it true?** Not "is it inspiring" — is it actually true?
2. **Is it clean?** Would I be okay with someone I respect watching this?
3. **Is there a real ask?** Does it give the viewer something concrete to do, however small?

If the answer to any of these is no, the script goes back.

## Automatic rejection signals

The ethics check should reject or flag scripts that drift into:

- humiliation or contempt aimed at any person or group
- hate or hostility framed as "just being honest"
- clinical claims (medical, psychiatric, legal, financial) stated as fact
- conspiracy framing
- empty grindset clichés ("rise and grind", "sleep when you're dead", etc.)
- shame-driven or fear-driven hooks ("you'll end up alone / broke / forgotten if you don't…")
- vague affirmation with no concrete ask
- anything that treats the viewer as a mark

A flagged script is shown to the operator with the reason. A rejected script does not advance.
